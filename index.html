Background Concepts (Typing Mode)

1. Full Page Background

This applies the background image to the entire web page. The image covers the whole screen, stays centered, and does not repeat. Used when you want a single large image behind the entire website.

2. Hero Section With Overlay

A hero section is the big banner at the top of the page. It has a background image and a dark transparent overlay on top so that text becomes clearly readable. Mostly used for welcome headings.

3. Contain vs Cover

Cover: Fills the entire area, image may crop, looks clean and edge-to-edge. Best for banners or large backgrounds.

Contain: Shows the whole image without cropping, but empty space may appear. Best when you need the full image visible.

4. Repeat Background

A small image or pattern repeats like tiles to fill the element. Used for textures, designs, wallpapers, and decorative backgrounds.

5. Multiple Backgrounds

You can apply more than one background image at the same time. Example: gradient on top + image at the back. Useful for adding highlights, overlays, lighting effects, or shapes.
